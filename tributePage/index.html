Nikola Tesla

Inventor, Engineer, Genius
Nikola Tesla, shown with his famous Tesla Coil, was one of the greatest inventors of his time. His work contributed to many inventions that have reshaped our use and dependability on electricity.

    Nikola Tesla: Timeline of Events

  • 1856 - Born on July 10th in the village of Smiljan of the Austrian Empire.
  • 1875 - Enrolled at Austrian Polytechnic in Graz after graduating from the Higher Real Gymnasium in only three years studying calculus and electricity.
  • 1878 - Left Graz after he became addictied to gambling and dropped out of school, also cutting ties with his family.
  • 1881 - Moved to Budapest to work for the Budapest Telephone Exchange.
  • 1882 - Moved to Paris to work for the Continental Edison Company and also identified the magnetic induction field principle.
  • 1884 - Due to his work, Tesla was requested to emigrate to New York City, USA to work for Edison and his latest projects.
  • 1885 - Telsa stepped away from the Edison company after non payment for his work and decided to venture on his own.
  • 1887 - Cofounded the Tesla Electric Company with Alfred S. Brown, a Western Union superintendent, and New York attorney Charles F. Peck. Together they developed AC applications as opposed to Edison's DC applications.
  • 1891 - Tesla became a naturalized citizen of the United States and patented his famous Tesla Coil.
  • 1901 - Construction began on the Wardenclyffe Tower. Tesla believed that the tower could transmit electrical energy over long distances wirelessly, however the project was never completed.
  • 1934 - The New York Times published Tesla's particle beam research.
  • 1943 - Nikola passes away alone in room 3327 of the New Yorker Hotel.

    Nikola Tesla: Inventions and Contributions

  • 1886 - While experimenting with electromagnetic fields, Tesla noticed that these energitic fields could pass freely through materials. This eventually led to the discovery of X-rays.
  • 1887 - Improved and created a practical and working Induction Motor. This is hailed as the first successful AC motor.
  • 1888 - Contributed to Three-phase Electric Power. A common method of AC electric power generation, transmission, and distribution.
  • 1889 - Duplicated Hertz's experiments to produce "electrostatic thrusts", otherwise known as Wireless Telegraphy. This paves the way for the wireless transmission of radio and electromagnetic waves.
  • 1891 - Demonstrated and patented his famous Tesla Coil.
  • 1893 - Invented the controversial Violet Ray. A deviced used for electrotherapy.
  • 1896 - Invented and patented the Vacuum Variable Capacitor. These enhanced the quality of electrical components for handling currents of high frequency and potential.
  • 1898 - Came up with the first Remote Control. He demonstrated his remote controlled or "teleautomation" boat during the electrical exhibition at Madison Square Garden.
  • 1899 - Tested his famous Tesla Oscillator. Otherwise known as the Earthquake Machine which was believed to have the power to bring down buildings.
  • 1913 - Patented the bladeless centripetal flow Tesla Turbine.
  • 1930 - Rumor has it that this is when Tesla invented is infamous Death Ray.
